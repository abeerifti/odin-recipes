Haleem

Description:

A rich and hearty Middle Eastern porridge, haleem is made by slow-cooking a blend of wheat, barley, and meat until it reaches a creamy consistency, seasoned with spices, and garnished with fried onions and fresh herbs.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup of whole wheat, barley, or a combination of both
  • 500 grams of boneless chicken or mutton, cut into small pieces (you can also use beef or lentils for a vegetarian version)
  • 2 onions, finely chopped
  • 2 tablespoons of ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 teaspoon of red chili powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon of turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon of garam masala
  • Salt to taste
  • 4-5 cups of water (for cooking the grains and meat)
  • 2-3 tablespoons of cooking oil or ghee (clarified butter)
  • 2-3 green chilies, finely chopped (adjust to your spice preference)
  • Fresh coriander leaves (cilantro), chopped, for garnish
  • Fresh mint leaves, chopped, for garnish
  • Fried onions, for garnish (optional)
  • Lemon wedges, for serving
  • Ghee or clarified butter, for drizzling (optional)

Steps:

  1. Soak the Grains:
    • Soak 1 cup of whole wheat, barley, or a combination in water for 4-5 hours or overnight.
  2. Cook the Grains and Meat:
    • Boil the soaked grains, boneless chicken or mutton, onions, spices, and water for 2-3 hours until they're tender and fully cooked.
  3. Blend the Mixture:
    • Use a blender to turn the cooked mixture into a thick porridge-like consistency.
  4. Season and Sauté:
    • Sauté green chilies in oil or ghee until aromatic.
  5. Add the Blended Mixture:
    • Mix the sautéed chilies with the blended grain and meat mixture.
    • Cook for 15-20 minutes, allowing the haleem to thicken and the flavors to meld.
  6. Garnish and Serve:
    • Garnish with coriander, mint, and fried onions if desired.
    • Serve hot with lemon wedges.
    • Optionally, drizzle with ghee for extra flavor.
