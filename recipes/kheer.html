Kheer

Description:

A creamy and comforting Pakistani rice pudding, kheer is prepared with rice simmered in milk, sweetened with sugar, and flavored with cardamom and saffron, making it a delightful dessert loved across the subcontinent.

Ingredients:

  • Rice - 1/2 cup
  • Milk - 4 cups
  • Sugar - 1/2 cup (adjust to taste)
  • Cardamom pods - 4-5, lightly crushed
  • Saffron strands - a pinch (soaked in warm milk)
  • Almonds - 10-12, sliced or slivered
  • Pistachios - 10-12, sliced or slivered
  • Raisins - 2 tablespoons
  • Ghee (clarified butter) - 1 tablespoon
  • Optional garnishes: Chopped nuts like almonds or pistachios

Steps:

  1. Wash and rinse 1/2 cup of rice.
  2. In a large pot, combine the rice and 4 cups of milk, and bring to a boil.
  3. Reduce the heat to low and simmer, stirring occasionally, until the rice is cooked and the kheer thickens (about 30-40 minutes).
  4. Add 1/2 cup of sugar (adjust to taste), crushed cardamom pods, and a pinch of saffron soaked in warm milk. Stir well.
  5. In a separate pan, heat 1 tablespoon of ghee and lightly fry the almonds, pistachios, and raisins. Add them to the kheer.
  6. Simmer for a few more minutes, allowing the flavors to meld.
  7. Remove from heat, and once it cools, optionally garnish with chopped nuts such as almonds or pistachios
  8. Chill and serve the kheer cold. Enjoy!
