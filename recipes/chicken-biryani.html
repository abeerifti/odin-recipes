Chicken Biryani

Description:

A fragrant and flavorful South Asian dish, chicken biryani combines succulent chicken pieces with aromatic rice, spices, and herbs, creating a mouthwatering one-pot meal.

Ingredients:

For the Marinade:

  • 500 grams (about 1.1 pounds) of chicken (bone-in pieces)
  • 1 cup plain yogurt (about 240 ml)
  • 2 tablespoons ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon garam masala
  • 2 tablespoons lemon juice
  • Salt to taste

For the Rice:

  • 2 cups Basmati rice (about 400 grams), soaked for 30 minutes
  • 4-5 cups water for cooking the rice
  • 2-3 green cardamom pods
  • 2-3 cloves
  • 1-inch cinnamon stick
  • Salt to taste

For the Biryani:

  • 3 tablespoons vegetable oil or ghee (clarified butter)
  • 2 large onions, thinly sliced
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 1/2 cup fresh coriander leaves (cilantro), chopped
  • 1/2 cup fresh mint leaves, chopped
  • Saffron strands soaked in 2 tablespoons of warm milk (for garnish)
  • Fried onions (for garnish, optional)
  • 1-2 bay leaves
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 4-5 green cardamom pods
  • 4-5 cloves
  • 2-inch cinnamon stick
  • 1-2 dried bay leaves
  • 1/2 teaspoon black peppercorns

Steps:

  1. Marinate the Chicken:
    • Combine chicken pieces, yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, red chili powder, turmeric powder, garam masala, lemon juice, and salt in a bowl.
    • Mix well, ensuring the chicken is coated with the marinade. Marinate for at least 30 minutes (longer for better flavor), ideally in the refrigerator.
  2. Prepare the Rice:
    • Wash and soak the Basmati rice for 30 minutes, then drain.
    • In a large pot, bring 4-5 cups of water to a boil and add the rice, along with green cardamom pods, cloves, cinnamon stick, and salt.
    • Cook the rice until it's about 70% cooked (still slightly firm). Drain and set aside.
  3. Cook the Chicken:
    • In a large, heavy-bottomed pot or biryani pot, heat vegetable oil or ghee over medium heat.
    • Add sliced onions and sauté until they turn golden brown. Remove some for garnish, if desired.
    • Add bay leaves, cumin seeds, green cardamom, cloves, cinnamon stick, dried bay leaves, and black peppercorns. Sauté for a minute until aromatic.
    • Add marinated chicken and cook until it changes color and is partially cooked.
    • Add chopped tomatoes and cook until they soften.
    • Layer half of the cooked rice over the chicken.
    • Sprinkle half of the chopped coriander and mint leaves over the rice.
    • Repeat the layering process with the remaining rice, coriander, and mint leaves.
    • Drizzle the saffron milk over the top and garnish with fried onions, if desired.
    • Cover the pot with a lid and cook on low heat for about 20-25 minutes to allow the flavors to meld and the chicken to cook through.
  4. Serve the Chicken Biryani hot, garnished with fresh mint and coriander leaves if desired. Enjoy!
